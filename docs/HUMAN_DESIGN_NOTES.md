## Human Design Notes

This file exists to keep Aeterna from drifting back into generic AI-shaped product design.

### What stronger human product design usually does

- Gives each screen a specific job instead of repeating one marketing hero everywhere.
- Prioritizes one primary action or one primary question per screen.
- Uses plain language before “product language”.
- Keeps high-value signals near the control that depends on them.
- Makes empty states feel intentional, not unfinished.
- Shows system truth honestly, even when the truth is “not ready yet”.
- Uses contrast and spacing to establish hierarchy instead of outlining everything equally.
- Cuts decorative pills, labels, and copy that do not help a decision.

### What AI-slanted product design tends to do

- Repeats the same hero banner across every page.
- Treats every card as equally important.
- Uses soft, generic copy that sounds correct but says little.
- Hides weak product states behind polished surfaces.
- Adds metadata chips everywhere because they look structured.
- Prefers symmetry and repetition even when the product needs hierarchy.

### Rules for Aeterna

- Dashboard is for operational posture, not product marketing.
- Optimization is for session control, not generic “AI recommendations”.
- Security is for boundaries and trust, not fear-based copy.
- Models must state what is ONNX, what is fallback, and what is not ready.
- Activity must prove what changed and how to undo it.
- Settings must explain product policy, not just expose toggles.

### Current design standard

- Fewer claims.
- Stronger hierarchy.
- More specific copy.
- More task-specific headers.
- Reversible actions stay visually tied to rollback and trust.
